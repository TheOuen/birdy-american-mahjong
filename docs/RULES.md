# American Mahjong — Complete Rules & Game Guide

Source: american_mahjong_rules_1.pdf

---

## 1. Overview

American Mahjong is a tile-based game typically played by four players. Unlike traditional Chinese variants, American Mahjong uses a standardised card published annually by the National Mah Jongg League (NMJL), which defines all valid winning hands for that year. Players draw and discard tiles to complete one of the hands listed on the card.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Players** | 4 (standard) |
| **Tiles** | 152 tiles total (including jokers) |
| **Goal** | Complete a winning hand from the current year's NMJL card |
| **Turn order** | Counter-clockwise |

---

## 2. The Tiles

American Mahjong uses 152 tiles:

### Suit Tiles (108 tiles)
- **Bamboo (Bam):** numbered 1-9, four of each = 36 tiles
- **Characters (Crak):** numbered 1-9, four of each = 36 tiles
- **Circles (Dot):** numbered 1-9, four of each = 36 tiles

### Wind Tiles (16 tiles)
- East, West, North, South — four of each = 16 tiles

### Dragon Tiles (12 tiles)
- Red Dragon (Chun), Green Dragon (Hatsu), White Dragon (Haku) — four of each = 12 tiles

### Flower Tiles (8 tiles)
- Numbered 1-4, two sets = 8 tiles

### Joker Tiles (8 tiles)
- 8 jokers — unique to American Mahjong
- Jokers can substitute for any tile in a group of three or more identical tiles
- Jokers cannot be used in pairs or single-tile positions
- A player may swap a joker on the table with the tile it represents on their own turn

## 3. Setup

### Determining the East Wind (Dealer)
1. Shuffle all tiles face down and build them into four walls, each 19 tiles long and two tiles high
2. Roll dice to determine the starting dealer — the player with the highest roll becomes East
3. East deals first and the role rotates counter-clockwise each round

### Building the Wall
4. Each player builds a wall of 38 tiles (19 stacks of 2) in front of them
5. The four walls are pushed together to form a square

### Breaking the Wall
6. The dealer rolls two dice
7. Count that number of stacks from the right end of the dealer's wall
8. Dealing begins from that point, moving counter-clockwise

### The Deal
9. The dealer takes 4 tiles at a time, going counter-clockwise around the table
10. This continues until each player has 12 tiles
11. Each player then takes one more tile — the dealer takes two (14 total), all others take one (13 total)
12. The dealer discards one tile to begin play

## 4. Gameplay

### On Your Turn
Each turn follows this sequence:
1. Pick up a tile — either from the wall or by claiming a discard
2. Arrange your hand as desired
3. Discard one tile face up to the centre, announcing it aloud

### Claiming Discards
Any player may claim a discard, but the following priority rules apply:
- **Mahjong** (winning hand): any player may claim at any time — this takes priority over all other claims
- **Any other claim:** the next player in turn order has first priority; if they pass, any other player may claim

To claim a discard, the player must:
1. Call out what they are claiming (e.g. "Pung," "Kong," or "Mahjong")
2. Expose the completed group face up on the table
3. Discard a tile to complete their turn

### Groups
- **Pung:** three identical tiles (e.g. three 3 Bam)
- **Kong:** four identical tiles (e.g. four 9 Dot)
- **Quint:** five identical tiles — possible only when jokers are used
- **Sextet:** six identical tiles — possible only when jokers are used

Note: A pair is two identical tiles. Pairs cannot contain jokers.

### Exposed vs. Concealed Tiles
- Tiles claimed from a discard are placed face up (exposed) on the table
- Tiles drawn from the wall remain concealed in hand
- Some winning hands require a fully concealed hand — check the NMJL card carefully

## 5. Winning

### Calling Mahjong
A player wins by completing a hand that exactly matches one of the patterns on the current year's NMJL card. To win:
1. Declare "Mahjong"
2. Expose your entire hand face up
3. The hand is verified against the card by all players

### The NMJL Card
The National Mah Jongg League publishes a new card each year listing all valid winning hands. Each hand is grouped into sections (e.g. consecutive runs, winds and dragons, etc.).

Key points:
- The platform must support the current year's card and allow for annual card updates
- Each hand shows the exact tile combination and its point value
- Hands marked with "C" require a fully concealed hand

### Scoring
- The winning hand's point value is printed on the NMJL card (typically 25-60 points)
- Each losing player pays the winner the value of the hand
- If the winning tile came from a discard, only the player who discarded it pays — and pays double
- If the winner drew their own winning tile from the wall (self-draw), all three opponents pay the full value
- A joker-free winning hand may command a premium (house rules vary)

### Dead Hand
A player's hand is declared dead (and they are out for the round) if:
- They expose tiles that do not match any valid hand on the card
- They claim a discard incorrectly
- They have the wrong number of tiles

## 6. Special Rules

### The Charleston
The Charleston is a pre-game tile exchange that happens before the first discard:
1. **First Right:** each player passes three tiles face down to the player on their right
2. **First Across:** each player passes three tiles face down to the player across from them
3. **First Left:** each player passes three tiles face down to the player on their left

A second Charleston may follow if all four players agree:
4. **Second Left, Second Across, Second Right**

After the Charlestons, a player may optionally perform a courtesy pass of 1-3 tiles to the player across.

Rules: you may never pass back a tile you just received in the same direction. Blind passes (passing without looking) are allowed.

### Joker Rules (Summary)
- Jokers may substitute for any tile in a set of 3 or more identical tiles
- Jokers cannot be used in pairs (exactly 2 tiles) or as a single tile
- On your turn, you may swap a joker from an exposed set on the table with the tile it represents — you must hold that tile in your hand
- You may not then discard the tile you used to swap

### No Mahjong — Wall Game
If all tiles are drawn from the wall without any player winning, the round is declared a draw (wall game). No points are exchanged and the same dealer deals again.

## 7. Etiquette & Common House Rules

- Announce discards clearly and audibly
- Do not touch a discard until you have declared your claim
- Do not advise other players on their hands
- The NMJL card must be visible to all players at all times
- Disputes should be resolved by referring to the NMJL card or a neutral player vote

Common optional rules (confirm with your player community):
- Extra payments for joker-free winning hands
- Allowing or disallowing blind passes during the Charleston
- Varying the number of Charlestons (one or two)

## 8. Quick Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Bam** | Bamboo suit tiles (1-9) |
| **Crak** | Character suit tiles (1-9) |
| **Dot** | Circle suit tiles (1-9) |
| **Charleston** | Pre-game tile exchange unique to American Mahjong |
| **Dead Hand** | An invalid hand — player is out for that round |
| **East** | The dealer; rotates counter-clockwise each round |
| **Joker** | Wild tile; substitutes for any tile in a group of 3+ |
| **Kong** | A set of four identical tiles |
| **Mahjong** | Completing a winning hand; also the name of the game |
| **NMJL** | National Mah Jongg League — publishes the annual winning hand card |
| **Pung** | A set of three identical tiles |
| **Self-draw** | Drawing your winning tile from the wall (not a discard) |
| **Wall Game** | A draw — all tiles exhausted with no winner |
